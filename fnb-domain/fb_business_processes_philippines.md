## Philippines F&B Overview

### Market Characteristics

**Demographics & Economy**:
- **Population**: 115 million (2026)
- **Young population**: 64% under 35 years
- **Urbanization**: 48% (Metro Manila, Cebu, Davao)
- **GDP per capita**: ~$3,900 USD (lower-middle income)
- **Growth**: 5-6% annually
- **Overseas Filipino Workers (OFW)**: Remittances drive consumption

**F&B Landscape**:
- **Street food culture**: 60%+ eat street food regularly
- **Affordable pricing**: Meals from ‚Ç±30-100 ($0.55-$1.80 USD)
- **Rice-centric**: Rice with every meal
- **Jollibee dominance**: Local fast-food chain beats McDonald's
- **Carinderia/turo-turo**: Point-and-eat eateries ubiquitous
- **Merienda culture**: Afternoon snack time (3-5pm) critical
- **Sari-sari store integration**: Neighborhood convenience stores sell food

**Unique Filipino F&B Aspects**:
- **"Kamayan" style**: Eating with hands (boodle fights)
- **Sweet everything**: Sweet spaghetti, sweet hotdogs, sweet bread
- **Mix culture**: Spanish-American-Asian fusion
- **Fiesta mentality**: Food abundance = hospitality
- **Balikbayan effect**: OFWs returning = spending spike
- **Typhoon dependency**: Weather drastically affects business
- **Island variations**: 7,641 islands = diverse regional cuisines

## Business Models

### 1. Carinderia (Small Neighborhood Eatery)

**What is it**: Small family-run eatery, display-service style

**Typical Setup**:
```
Location: Residential areas, near markets
Size: 20-40 sq meters (tiny!)
Seating: 5-10 tables, plastic chairs
Display: Glass case with pre-cooked viands
Kitchen: Small prep area, gas stove
Staff: Owner + 1-2 family members
```

**Operating Model**:
```
Hours: 6am-8pm (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
Peak Times:
- Breakfast: 6-8am
- Lunch: 11am-1pm (PEAK!)
- Merienda: 3-5pm
- Dinner: 6-8pm

Service Style:
- Customer looks at display
- Points at viands (ulam)
- Rice + 1-2 viands plated
- Eat at table
- Pay after eating (honor system)
```

**Pricing Structure**:
```
Plain Rice: ‚Ç±10-15
+ Vegetable (gulay): ‚Ç±15-25
+ Chicken (manok): ‚Ç±30-50
+ Pork (baboy): ‚Ç±35-60
+ Fish (isda): ‚Ç±40-70

Typical meal: ‚Ç±50-90 per person
```

**Revenue Model**:

```
Small Carinderia:
Daily Customers: 80-150
Average Bill: ‚Ç±65
Daily Revenue: ‚Ç±5,200-9,750
Monthly Revenue (26 days): ‚Ç±135,200-253,500 ($2,400-4,500 USD)

Costs:
- Ingredients: 40% = ‚Ç±54,000-101,400
- Rent: ‚Ç±8,000-15,000 (6-11%)
- Labor: Family (owner works) or ‚Ç±6,000-10,000
- Utilities: ‚Ç±3,000-5,000
- LPG gas: ‚Ç±2,000-3,000
- Misc: ‚Ç±2,000
Total Costs: ‚Ç±75,000-136,400

Net Profit: ‚Ç±60,200-117,100/month (45-46%)
Owner Income: ‚Ç±66,000-127,000 if working
```

**Menu Staples**:
- Adobo (chicken/pork in soy-vinegar)
- Sinigang (sour soup)
- Menudo (pork with liver, tomato)
- Giniling (ground meat)
- Tortang talong (eggplant omelet)
- Pakbet (mixed vegetables)

### 2. Turo-Turo (Point-Point Eatery)

**What is it**: "Point-point" - cafeteria-style, choose-your-viand

**Similar to Carinderia but**:
- Faster service (office lunch crowd)
- More variety (12-20 viands vs 6-10)
- Higher turnover
- Less seating (often takeaway)
- Urban locations (business districts)

**Operating Model**:
```
Prime Locations:
- Near offices, government buildings
- Business districts (Makati, BGC, Ortigas)
- Universities

Peak Hour: 11:30am-1pm (crush lunch!)
Service Speed: 2-3 minutes per customer

Takeaway: 40-60% (styrofoam containers)
Dine-in: 40-60%
```

**Economics** (Office District Turo-Turo):
```
Daily Customers: 200-400 (lunch focus)
Average Spend: ‚Ç±75
Daily Revenue: ‚Ç±15,000-30,000
Monthly: ‚Ç±390,000-780,000 ($6,900-13,800)

Costs:
- Ingredients: 38%
- Rent: ‚Ç±30,000-60,000 (higher, prime location)
- Labor: 3-4 staff = ‚Ç±15,000-24,000
- Utilities: ‚Ç±5,000
- Styrofoam containers: ‚Ç±8,000-12,000
- Misc: ‚Ç±3,000

## Operations & Workflows

### Carinderia Daily Routine

**Morning Prep** (4am-10am):
```
4:00am - Wake up, go to palengke (wet market)
5:00am - Buy fresh ingredients:
  * Meat (chicken, pork, fish)
  * Vegetables (kangkong, sitaw, kalabasa)
  * Rice (25-50 kg sacks)
  * Condiments

6:00am - Return, start cooking
6:30am - First batch ready (breakfast items)
7:00am - Open for breakfast customers
8:00am - Start cooking lunch viands
10:00am - All lunch dishes ready, in display

Cooking Done:
- 6-10 viands prepared
- Large batches (cooking for 30-50 servings each)
- Keep warm in display case
```

**Service Hours**:
```
10:30am-2pm: LUNCH RUSH
- 60% of daily revenue
- Rapid serving (1-2 min per customer)
- Constant replenishment of rice

2pm-3pm: Slow period, cleanup

3pm-5pm: Merienda (if offered)

5:30pm-8pm: Dinner service
- 25% of daily revenue
- More relaxed

8pm: Close, cleanup, prep for tomorrow
```

### Typhoon/Weather Impact

**Critical Business Factor**:
```
Sunny Day:
- Street vendors thrive
- Full customer turnout
- Normal revenue

Rainy Day:
- 30-50% revenue drop
- Street food vendors close
- Indoor carinderias gain

Typhoon Day:
- Everything closed
- Zero revenue
- Stock prep needed for after-storm demand

Typhoon Season: June-November
- Plan for 5-10 zero-revenue days
- Cash reserve critical
- Insurance rare (too expensive)
```

**Cash Dominant**:
```
Cash: 75-80% of transactions (2026)

- Carinderia customers prefer cash
- Daily wage workers (cash income)

```

```

## Challenges & BI Solutions

### Philippines F&B Pain Points

#### 1. Typhoon/Weather Dependency

**Problem**:
- Typhoon = zero revenue day
- 5-10 days/year completely wasted
- Can't plan, erratic weather

**BI Solution**:
```
Dashboard: "Weather Impact Tracker"
Track:
- Revenue on rainy vs sunny days
- Typhoon season patterns (June-Nov worse)
- PAGASA (weather agency) forecast integration
- Ingredient purchasing adjustments

Recommendation:
- "Typhoon Ompong forecasted this week ‚Üí order 30% less"
- "Build cash reserve: ‚Ç±15,000 for 3 zero-revenue days"

Risk Planning: Set aside 10% monthly profit for weather buffer
```

---

#### 2. Lunch Hour Concentration

**Problem**:
- 60% revenue in 2 hours (11am-1pm)
- Can't serve everyone (queue too long)
- Lost sales due to capacity

**BI Solution**:
```
Dashboard: "Lunch Rush Optimizer"
Analyze:
- Orders by 10-minute blocks
- Queue abandonment (people leave if queue too long)
- Viand sell-out times

Recommendations:
- "Adobo sold out by 12:15pm = lost sales of ‚Ç±800"
- "Prep 20% more for top 3 viands"
- "ROI of hiring lunch-hour helper: 180%"
```
