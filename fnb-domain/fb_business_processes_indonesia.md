## Indonesia F&B Overview

### Market Characteristics

**Demographics & Economy**:
- **Population**: 275 million (4th largest globally)
- **Muslim majority**: 87% Muslim (largest Muslim population worldwide)
- **Young population**: 52% under 30 years
- **Urbanization**: 56% (Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, Medan)
- **GDP per capita**: ~$4,800 USD (lower-middle income)
- **Growth**: 5% annually (emerging market)
- **Archipelago**: 17,000+ islands, huge diversity

**F&B Landscape**:
- **Warung culture**: Small roadside eateries ubiquitous
- **Halal essential**: 95%+ of market must be halal
- **Ultra-affordable**: Meals from Rp 10,000-30,000 ($0.65-$2 USD)
- **Rice-based**: Nasi (rice) with everything
- **Street food dominant**: 65% eat street food regularly
- **Goek/Grab dominance**: Digital platforms massive
- **Regional diversity**: Java ‚â† Bali ‚â† Sumatra cuisines

**Unique Indonesian F&B Aspects**:
- **Nasi Padang**: Display-service Sumatran food (pay for what you eat)
- **Warung tegal (Warteg)**: Ultra-cheap Javanese workers' eatery
- **Kaki lima**: Mobile street food carts (5 feet = street vendor)
- **Sambal obsession**: Chili sauce with everything
- **Gorengan**: Fried snack culture (afternoon)
- **Ramadan impact**: Fasting month = 40-60% revenue spike
- **OVO/GoPay**: Digital wallets more popular than credit cards

## Business Models

### 1. Warung (Small Roadside Eatery)

**What is it**: Small family-run restaurant, Indonesian staple

**Characteristics**:
```
Location: Roadside, residential areas
Size: 15-40 sq meters
Seating: 8-15 plastic tables
Service: Owner + family members
Menu: 10-20 Indonesian dishes
Hours: 6am-9pm (all-day service)
```

**Typical Setup**:
```
Investment: Rp 15-40 million initial
Equipment:
- Gas stoves (2-4 burners)
- Display case (warm food display)
- Plastic tables + stools
- Basic kitchen equipment
- Cash drawer

Rent: Rp 2-5 million/month (outside Jakarta)
```

**Pricing**:
```
Nasi putih (plain rice): Rp 3,000-5,000
+ Tempe/tahu goreng: Rp 3,000-5,000
+ Sayur (vegetables): Rp 5,000-8,000
+ Ayam goreng (fried chicken): Rp 12,000-20,000
+ Ikan (fish): Rp 15,000-25,000

Average meal: Rp 15,000-25,000 per person
```

**Revenue Model**:

```
Medium Warung (Java):
Daily Customers: 100-180
Average Spend: Rp 20,000
Daily Revenue: Rp 2,000,000-3,600,000
Monthly Revenue (26 days): Rp 52M-93.6M ($3,250-$5,850 USD)

Costs:
- Ingredients: 40% = Rp 20.8M-37.4M
- Rent: Rp 3M-5M (6-8%)
- Labor (helper): Rp 2.5M-4M
- LPG gas: Rp 1.5M-2.5M
- Utilities: Rp 800k-1.2M
- Misc: Rp 500k
Total Costs: Rp 29.1M-50.6M (56-54%)

Net Profit: Rp 22.9M-43M/month (44-46%)
Owner Income: Rp 25.4M-47M if working ($1,590-$2,940)
```

**Menu Staples**:
- Nasi goreng (fried rice)
- Mie goreng (fried noodles)
- Soto ayam (chicken soup)
- Ayam/ikan goreng (fried chicken/fish)
- Tempe/tahu goreng (fried tempeh/tofu)
- Gado-gado (vegetable salad with peanut sauce)

### 2. Warung Tegal (Warteg)

**What is it**: Ultra-cheap Javanese workers' eatery (Tegal city origin)

**Characteristics**:
- **Cheapest dining option**: Rp 10,000-18,000 full meal
- **Working class**: Construction workers, drivers, students
- **Simple setup**: Very basic, often outdoor seating
- **Pre-cooked display**: Choose viands, vendor plates
- **Family-operated**: Husband-wife team common

**Warteg Economics**:
```
Ultra-Low Pricing:
Rice: Rp 2,500
Tempe: Rp 2,000
Sayur: Rp 3,000
Ayam: Rp 8,000
Total: Rp 15,500 average

High Volume Strategy:
Daily Customers: 150-300
Daily Revenue: Rp 2.3M-4.6M
Monthly: Rp 60M-120M

Costs:
- Ultra-lean ingredients: 42%
- Rent: Rp 1.5M-3M (very cheap locations)
- Family operates (no labor cost!)
- LPG: Rp 1.5M
- Utilities: Rp 500k

Net Profit: 35-40%
= Rp 21M-48M/month
But: Owner works 14 hours/day!
```

### 3. Nasi Padang Restaurant

**What is it**: Sumatran (Padang) style display-service restaurant

**Unique System**:
```
Service Flow:
1. Customer sits
2. Waiter brings 10-15 small plates to table
3. All dishes displayed
4. Customer eats what they want
5. Waiter counts what was touched
6. Charges only for eaten dishes!
7. Untouched dishes returned to kitchen

"Hidangan" system:
- Rendang (beef curry)
- Gulai (curry)
- Sambal (chili)
- Ikan (fish)
- Telur balado (spicy egg)
- Vegetables
```

**Pricing Model**:
```
Rice: Rp 5,000 (base)
+ Each dish touched: Rp 8,000-25,000
+ Specialty (rendang, ikan): Rp 15,000-35,000

Average Bill: Rp 30,000-50,000 per person
Higher than regular warung!
```

**Economics** (Nasi Padang Medium):
```
Daily Covers: 120-200
Average: Rp 38,000
Daily Revenue: Rp 4.5M-7.6M
Monthly: Rp 117M-198M

Costs:
- Ingredients: 38% (higher quality)
- Rent: Rp 8M-15M
- Labor: 4-6 staff = Rp 8M-15M
- Utilities: Rp 2M
- Misc: Rp 2M

Net Profit: 30-35%
= Rp 35M-69M/month ($2,190-$4,315)
```

**Success Factor**: Quality! Padang food must be authentic, properly spiced

## Operations & Workflows

### Warung Daily Routine

**Morning Prep** (4am-9am):
```
4:00am - Wake up, go to pasar tradisional (traditional market)
5:00am - Buy fresh ingredients:
  * Ayam/ikan (chicken/fish)
  * Sayuran (vegetables: kangkung, bayam, kol)
  * Tempe, tahu (always!)
  * Beras (rice - 25kg sacks)
  * Sambal ingredients (cabai, tomat, terasi)

6:00am - Return, start cooking
6:30am - First dishes ready
7:00am - Open for breakfast customers
8:00am - Continue cooking lunch dishes
9:00am - All dishes in display case
```

**Service**:
```
9:30am-2pm: LUNCH RUSH
- 60% of daily revenue
- Office workers, construction workers
- Rapid turnover (eat and go)

2pm-3pm: Slow, rest time

3pm-5pm: Gorengan time (afternoon snack)

5:30pm-8:30pm: Dinner service
- Families, evening customers
- 25-30% of daily revenue

8:30pm: Close, cleanup
```

## Front of House (FOH) & Back of House (BOH) Operations

### Indonesian FOH Roles & Workflows

**Warung/Traditional FOH** (Minimal, family-run):
```
Roles:
1. Owner - Takes orders, serves, cashier (everything!)
2. Owner's wife/husband - Cooking + serving
3. Helper (if busy) - Rp 2.5M-3.5M/month
   - Wash dishes
   - Serve water
   - Clear tables

Service Flow (Warung):
1. Customer enters, sits anywhere
2. Owner comes with menu (or customer knows already!)
3. Order placed verbally
4. Owner writes on paper scrap (not POS!)
5. Owner shouts order to kitchen (wife/husband)
6. 

Food served 5-10 min later
7. Customer asks for bill (verbal total!)
8. Cash payment (rarely digital for cheap warungs)
9. Customer leaves, owner clears table

Data Generated: Mental notes! (no system)
```

**Modern Restaurant FOH** (KFC, Solaria, etc.):
```
Roles:
1. Cashier (Kasir) - Rp 3.5M-4.5M/month
   - Take orders at counter
   - POS system
   - Upsell (combo, drinks)
   - Process payment

2. Server (Waitress/Waiter) - Rp 3M-4M/month
   - Table service
   - Deliver food
   - Clear tables
   - Handle complaints

3. Floor Manager - Rp 5M-7M/month
   - Supervise staff
   - Handle escalations
   - Monitor service quality

Service Flow (Mall Restaurant):
1. Greeter seats customer (if table service)
2. Server greets, gives menu
3. Order taken (written on order pad or tablet)
4. Order sent to kitchen (POS or kitchen display)
5. Food prepared (15-25 min)
6. Server delivers food
7. Check-back during meal
8. Bill requested, processed
9. Payment (cash, GoPay, OVO, credit card)
10. Table cleared, reset

Data Generated:
- Server sales performance
- Table turnover time
- Payment method split
- Upsell success rate
```

## Supply Chain & Inventory Management

### Indonesian Supply Chain Models

**Traditional Warung**:
```
Daily Sourcing (Pasar Tradisional):

Morning routine:
1. Wake 4am
2. Go to pasar 4:30am
3. Shop 5am-6:30am
4. Return with ingredients
5. Cook same day (ultra-fresh!)

Advantages:
- Freshest ingredients daily
- No inventory waste (cook what you buy)
- Cash flow simple (buy today, sell today)
- Flexibility (adjust menu based on price/availability)

Disadvantages:
- No bulk discounts
- Price volatility (pasar prices change daily!)
- Labor intensive (daily market trips)
- Weather dependent (rain = higher prices)

Inventory: Minimal!
- Rice: 3-4 days supply (25kg sack)
- Oil, salt, spices: Weekly purchase
- Fresh food: Zero inventory (daily purchase)
```

**Modern Chain Restaurant**:
```
Centralized Sourcing:

Supply Chain:
1. Central Warehouse (Jakarta, Surabaya)
2. Bulk purchasing from suppliers
3. Daily/weekly delivery to outlets
4. Standardized portions

Example (Solaria):
- Supplier contracts (chicken, vegetables)
- Predictable pricing (less volatility)
- Quality control at warehouse
- Distribution trucks to 150+ outlets

Inventory Management:
- 3-7 days stock on-site
- PAR levels (minimum inventory)
- Automated reorder (POS integrated)
- FIFO (First In First Out)

Cost Savings:
- Bulk discount: 15-25% vs pasar
- Labor savings: No daily market trips
- Waste reduction: Better forecasting
- Standardization: Consistent quality

Trade-off:
- Less fresh (2-5 days old vs same-day)
- Acceptable for chain (customers expect consistency, not ultra-fresh)
```

### Inventory Challenges (Indonesia-Specific)

**1. Pasar Price Volatility**:
```
Example - Chicken prices:
Mon: Rp 36,000/kg
Tue: Rp 42,000/kg (+17%!)
Wed: Rp 38,000/kg

Why:
- Weather (rain = supply chain disruption)
- Religion (Friday prayer day, less supply)
- Seasonality (Ramadan = spike)

Warung Response:
- Adjust menu prices daily (flexible!)
- OR: Accept lower margin
- OR: Substitute (use tempe instead of chicken)

Chain Response:
- Supplier contracts (lock in price)
- Absorb volatility (stable menu prices)
```

**2. Ramadan Inventory Surge**:
```
Normal Month: Rp 350k ingredients/day
Ramadan: Rp 550k ingredients/day (+57%!)

Buka Puasa Rush (6pm):
- Need 2√ó normal stock
- Risk: If under-order = sold out (lost revenue!)
- Risk: If over-order = waste (food can't hold overnight)

Smart Planning:
- Historical data (last year's Ramadan)
- Daily adjustments (Day 1 sold out? Order more Day 2)
```

### Customer Service (Indonesian Context)

**Service Expectations**:
```
Formal vs Casual:

Warung: Very casual
- No "Selamat datang" (welcome) needed
- Owner talks while serving (personal!)
- Okay to share tables if crowded
- Very forgiving (mistakes forgiven easily)

Fine Dining: Formal (Western-style)
- "Selamat malam, silakan duduk" (greeting)
- Napkin on lap
- Server attentive but not intrusive
- Complaints handled immediately

Cultural Notes:
- Indonesians avoid confrontation
- Won't complain directly (just won't return!)
- Good service = quiet, efficient, respectful
- Over-friendly can be seen as pushy (vs USA where it's encouraged)
```

## Staff Management (Indonesian Labor Market)

### Hiring & Training

**Labor Market Reality**:
```
Challenges:
- High turnover (30-40% annually!)
- Young workforce (18-25 years, first job)
- Limited formal training (learn on job)
- Low English proficiency (outside Jakarta)

Typical Background:
- High school graduate (SMA)
- Village to city migration (cari kerja = find work)
- Lives in kost (boarding house, Rp 800k-1.5M/month)
- Sends money home to family (remittances)

Warung Hiring:
- Family first! (nephew, cousin)
- Word of mouth (neighbor's daughter)
- No formal interview
- Trial period (1-2 weeks, see if fits)
- Pay: Rp 2.5M-3.5M + meals

Modern Restaurant Hiring:
- Job posting (online, walk-in)
- Interview (basic)
- Training period (1-2 weeks)
- Uniform provided
- Pay: Rp 3.5M-5M + BPJS (health insurance)
```

---

### Labor Costs & Regulations

**Minimum Wage** (UMR - Upah Minimum Regional):
```
Jakarta (2026): Rp 5,067,381/month
Surabaya: Rp 4,783,000/month
Bandung: Rp 3,925,000/month
Yogyakarta: Rp 2,153,970/month (cheapest!)

Actual F&B Wages:
- Entry-level: Rp 3M-4M (below UMR often!)
- Mid-level: Rp 4.5M-6M
- Manager: Rp 7M-12M
- Chef: Rp 8M-15M

Reality:
- Many warungs pay below minimum (informal economy)
- Chains comply (avoid legal trouble)
- Trade-off: Lower wage but free meals worth Rp 600k-900k/month!
```

**BPJS (National Health Insurance)**:
```
Mandatory: Employers must provide
Cost: ~4% of salary (employer share)

Example:
Employee: Rp 4M salary
BPJS: Rp 160k/month (employer)
Total cost: Rp 4.16M

Warung Reality:
- Often don't provide (informal)
- Employee must buy own (Rp 42k-100k/month individual)

Chain Compliance:
- Must provide or face fines
- Adds 4-5% to labor costs
```

Works because:
- Simple menu (10-15 items)

- Low complexity
- Cash-based

```
Common POS:

- Order taking (tablet for servers)

Benefits:
- Faster service (no manual writing)

ROI Example:

Savings:
- Cashier error reduction: Rp 300k/month
- Inventory waste reduction: Rp 1M/month

Total: Rp 3.3M/month savings

Payback: 1.5 months!
```
