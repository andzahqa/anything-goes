## Kyoto F&B Overview

### Market Characteristics

**Kyoto City**:
- **Population**: 1.5 million (much smaller than Tokyo/Osaka!)
- **Ancient Capital**: 1,000+ years imperial capital (794-1868)
- **Cultural Heart**: 17 UNESCO World Heritage sites
- **Traditional Preservation**: Most traditional city in Japan
- **Tourism-Dependent**: 50M+ visitors/year (tourist-to-resident ratio 33:1!)
- **Seasonal Peak**: Cherry blossom (March-April), autumn leaves (Nov), Golden Week

**F&B Landscape Specifics**:
- **Kaiseki Capital**: Multi-course haute cuisine perfection
- **Most Expensive**: Premium above even Tokyo for traditional food
- **Seasonal Obsession**: Menu changes monthly (vs quarterly elsewhere)
- **Temple Cuisine**: Buddhist vegetarian (shojin ryori) unique here
- **Tea Ceremony**: Matcha culture, tea kaiseki
- **Preserved Traditions**: 300-year-old restaurants common
- **Machiya Dining**: Traditional wooden townhouse restaurants

**Unique Kyoto Characteristics**:
- **"Honne/Tatemae" Culture**: Surface politeness, hidden true feelings (more than Tokyo!)
- **Slow Food Movement**: Opposite of fast-paced Tokyo
- **Artisan Obsession**: Every ingredient sourced from specific craftsman
- **"Ichi-go ichi-e"**: Once-in-a-lifetime encounter (treasure each meal)
- **Discretion**: Best restaurants have no signs, reservations only

## Kyoto Cultural F&B Practices

### "Ichigen-san Okotowari" (First-Timer Refused)

**What is it**: No first-time customers policy

**Why Exists**:
```
Trust Culture:
- Kyoto values long-term relationships
- One-time tourists = unreliable
- No-shows extremely offensive
- Regulars = predictable income

How It Works:
- Need introduction from existing customer
- Or: Hotel concierge relationship
- Or: Visit multiple times (build familiarity gradually)

Modern Tension:
- UNESCO heritage = encourage tourism
- But: Traditional exclusivity = repel tourists
- Some adapt (online reservations)
- Others maintain (reputation over revenue)
```
