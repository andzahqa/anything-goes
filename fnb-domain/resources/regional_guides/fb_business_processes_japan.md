## Japan F&B Overview

### Market Characteristics

**Demographics & Economy**:
- **Population**: 125 million (declining)
- **Aging society**: 30% over 65 years (highest globally)
- **Urbanization**: 92% (Tokyo, Osaka, Nagoya mega-cities)
- **GDP per capita**: ~$40,000 USD (developed market)
- **Consumer spending**: High quality preference over quantity
- **Shrinking workforce**: Labor shortage crisis

**F&B Landscape**:
- **Premium pricing**: Â¥800-2,000 ($6-15 USD) typical meal
- **Quality obsession**: Perfection over profit
- **Specialization**: One dish mastered (ramen shop = only ramen)
- **Michelin dominance**: Tokyo has most Michelin stars globally (200+)
- **Konbini revolution**: 7-Eleven/Lawson = restaurant quality
- **Vending machines**: 5 million+ (hot food, cold food, everything!)
- **No tipping culture**: Service included, tipping offensive
- **Chain dominance**: Yoshinoya, Matsuya, Sukiya everywhere

**Unique Japanese F&B Aspects**:
- **Omotenashi**: Hospitality culture (anticipate needs)
- **Shokunin**: Craftsman spirit (lifetime dedication)
- **Itadakimasu/Gochisosama**: Meal gratitude rituals
- **Seasonal obsession**: Menu changes with seasons
- **Plastic food samples**: Display windows (sampuru)
- **Ticket vending machines**: Order before entering
- **Counter seating**: Chef interaction valued
- **Waste minimization**: "Mottainai" (waste is shameful)
