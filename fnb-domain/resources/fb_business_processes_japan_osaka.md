## Osaka F&B Overview

### Market Characteristics

**Osaka Metropolitan Area**:
- **Population**: 19 million (2nd largest after Tokyo)
- **Merchant Culture**: "Business first" mentality (vs Tokyo bureaucratic)
- **Casual Atmosphere**: Less formal, more friendly than Tokyo
- **Food Obsessed**: "Kuidaore" (eat until you collapse)
- **Value-Conscious**: Want good quality + fair price (not luxury for luxury's sake)
- **Second-City Pride**: Competitive with Tokyo, own identity

**F&B Landscape Specifics**:
- **Lower rent**: 40-50% cheaper than Tokyo equivalent locations
- **Lower prices**: 20-30% less than Tokyo for similar meals
- **Street food strong**: Takoyaki carts everywhere!
- **Casual dining dominant**: Less Michelin-chasing, more everyday deliciousness
- **Merchant negotiation**: Expect bargaining (even at restaurants!)
- **Dialect culture**: Kansai-ben (distinct from Tokyo dialect)

**"Kuidaore" Philosophy**:
```
Osaka vs Tokyo:
Tokyo: "Eat to live"
Osaka: "Live to eat!"

Spending Allocation:
Tokyo: Housing 35%, Food 25%
Osaka: Housing 25%, Food 35%

Osakaians spend MORE on food, less on housing!
```
